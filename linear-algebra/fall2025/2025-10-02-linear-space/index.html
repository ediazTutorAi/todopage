Linear Spaces

MATH 3330 · Dr. Esteban Diaz
Definition 4.1.1
Linear spaces (or vector spaces)
A linear space V is a set endowed with a rule for addition and a rule for scalar multiplication such that these operations satisfy the following eight rules (for all f, g, h in V and c, k in \(\mathbb{R}\))
1. \((f+g)+h=f+(g+h)\)
2. \(f+g=g+f\)
3. There exists a neutral element n in v such that \(f+n=f\) for all f in V. This n is unique and denoted by 0.
4. For each f in V, there exists a g in V such that \(f+g=0\). This g is unique and denoted by \((-f)\)
5. \(k(f+g)=kf+kg\)
6. \((c+k)f=cf+kf\)
7. \(c(kf)=(ck)f\)
8. \(1f=f\)
Examples of Linear Spaces
  • \(\mathbb{R}^{n}\) is the prototype of linear spaces
  • Let \(F(\mathbb{R},\mathbb{R})\) be the set of all functions from \(\mathbb{R}\) to \(\mathbb{R}\) with the operations $$(f+g)(x)=f(x)+g(x)$$ and $$(kf)(x)=kf(x)$$
  • \(\mathbb{R}^{n\times m}\) the set of all \(n \times m\) matrices, is a linear space. What is the neutral element?
  • The set of all infinite sequences of real numbers \((x_0,x_1,x_2,\cdots)\) with component wise addition and multiplication by scalar. What is the neutral element?
More examples of linear spaces
  • The linear equations in three unknowns $$ax+by+cz=d$$
  • A plane with no coordinates
  • Let \(\mathbb{C}=\{a+bi\text{ such that }a,b\in \mathbb{R},i^2=-1\}\) be the set of complex numbers
We say that an element \(f\) of a linear space is a linear combination of the elements \(f_1,f_2,\cdots,f_n\) if $$f=c_1f_1+c_2f_2+\cdots + c_nf_n$$ for some scalars \(c_1,c_2,\cdots,c_n\).
Definition 4.1.2
Subspaces
A subset W of a linear space W is called a subspace of V if:
  • W contains the neutral element 0 of V
  • W is closed under addition (if f and g are in W, then so is \(f+g\))
  • W is closed under scalar multiplication (if f is in W and k is a scalar, then kf is in W)
We can summarize parts b and c by saying that W is closed under linear combinations. Also notice that a linear subspace is also a linear space on its own, why we didn't prove that the eight properties hold?
Examples of subspaces
  • Show that the polynomials of degree \(\leq 2\), of the form \(f(x)=a+bx+cx^2\), are a subspace W of the space \(F(\mathbb{R},\mathbb{R})\) of all functions from \(\mathbb{R}\) to \(\mathbb{R}\)
  • Show that the differential functions form a subspace W of \(F(\mathbb{R},\mathbb{R}) \)
  • The smooth functions
  • P, the set of all polynomials
  • \(P_n\) the set of all polynomials of degree \(\leq n\)
