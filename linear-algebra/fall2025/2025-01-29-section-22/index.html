2.2 Linear Transformations in Geometry

MATH 3330 · Dr. Esteban Diaz
Example 1
Consider the matrices $A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 2\end{pmatrix}$, $B = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0\end{pmatrix}$, $C=\begin{pmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}$, $D=\begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0\end{pmatrix}$, $E=\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0.2 \\ 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}$, $F=\begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ 1 & 1\end{pmatrix}$.
Show the effect of each of these matrices on our standard letter L, and describe each transformation in words. Also, describe the effect of the scaling matrix: $$\begin{pmatrix}k&0\\0&k\end{pmatrix}$$
Definition 2.2.1
Orthogonal Projections
Consider a line L in the coordinate plane, running through the origin. Any vector $\vec{v}$ in $\mathbb{R}^2$ can be written uniquely as: $$\vec{x} = \vec{x}_\parallel + \vec{x}_\perp$$ where $\vec{x}_\parallel$ is parallel to L and $\vec{x}_\perp$ is perpendicular (orthogonal) to L.
The transformation $T(\vec{x}) = \vec{x}_\parallel$ is called the orthogonal projection of $\vec{x}$ onto L, often denoted as $proj_L(\vec{x})$. If $\vec{w}$ is a nonzero vector parallel to L, then: $$proj_L(\vec{x}) = \frac{\vec{x} \cdot \vec{w}}{\|\vec{w}\|^2} \vec{w}$$. In particular, if $\vec{u}=\begin{pmatrix}u_1\\u_2\end{pmatrix}$ is a unit vector parallel to L, then: $$proj_L(\vec{x})=(\vec{x}\cdot\vec{u})\vec{u}$$ The transformation $T(\vec{x})=proj_L(\vec{x})$ is linear, with matrix: $$A = \begin{pmatrix} u_1^2 & u_1u_2 \\ u_1u_2 & u_2^2 \end{pmatrix}$$ in the case of the unit vector $\vec{u}$.
Definition 2.2.2
Reflections
$$ref_L(\vec{x}) = 2proj_L(\vec{x}) - \vec{x}$$ The matrix of the reflection is of the form: $$A = \begin{pmatrix} a&b \\ b& -a \end{pmatrix}$$ where $a^2+b^2=1$. Conversely, any matrix of this form is the matrix of a reflection.
Theorem 2.2.3
Rotations
The matrix of a counterclockwise rotation by an angle $\theta$ is: $$A = \begin{pmatrix} \cos(\theta) & -\sin(\theta) \\ \sin(\theta) & \cos(\theta) \end{pmatrix}$$ Note that this matrix is of the form: $$A = \begin{pmatrix} a&-b \\ b& a \end{pmatrix}$$ where $a^2+b^2=1$. Conversely, any matrix of this form is the matrix of a rotation.
