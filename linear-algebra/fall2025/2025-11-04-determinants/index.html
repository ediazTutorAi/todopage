Definition 6.1.1
Determinant of a 3x3 matrix, in terms of the columns
If $A=[\vec{u} \vec{v} \vec{w}]$,then $$detA=\vec{u}\cdot(\vec{v}\times\vec{w})$$ A $3\times 3$ matrix A is invertible if (and only if) $det(A)\neq 0$
Theorem 6.1.2
Sarru's rule
To find the determinant of a $3\times 3$ matrix, extend the columns of the matrix and multiply accross the diagonals as show in class.

Definition 6.1.3
Patterns, inversions, and determinants
A patter in an nxn matrix A is a way to choose n entries of the matrix so that there is one chosen entry in each row and in each column of A
With a pattern P we associate the product of all its entries, denoted prod P.
Two entries in a pattern are said to be inverted if one of them is located to the right and above the other in the matrix
The signature of a pattern P is defined as $sgn(P)=(-1)^\text{number of inversions in P}$
