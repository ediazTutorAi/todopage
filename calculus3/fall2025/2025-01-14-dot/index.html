Dot and Cross Products

2.3 The Dot Product
Definition (Scalar Product)
For vectors $\mathbf{u}=\langle u_1,u_2,u_3\rangle$ and $\mathbf{v}=\langle v_1,v_2,v_3\rangle$: $$\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}=u_1v_1+u_2v_2+u_3v_3.$$ The result is a **scalar**. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
2.3 The Dot Product
Geometric Form (Angle)
If $\theta$ is the angle between nonzero vectors $\mathbf{u},\mathbf{v}$, $$\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}=||\mathbf{u}||\,||\mathbf{v}||\cos\theta.$$ Useful for finding angles. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
2.3 The Dot Product
Orthogonal Vectors
Vectors are **orthogonal** iff $$\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v}=0.$$ Useful perpendicularity test. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
2.3 The Dot Product
Projection
The projection of $\mathbf{v}$ onto $\mathbf{u}$ is $$\text{proj}_{\mathbf{u}}\,\mathbf{v}=\frac{\mathbf{v}\cdot\mathbf{u}}{\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{u}}\mathbf{u}.$$ (component of one vector along another) :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
2.4 The Cross Product
Definition (Vector Product)
For $\mathbf{u},\mathbf{v}\in\mathbb{R}^3$, $\mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v}$ is a **vector orthogonal** to both $\mathbf{u}$ & $\mathbf{v}$. Its direction follows the **right-hand rule**. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
2.4 The Cross Product
Determinant Formula
$$\mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v}=\begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k}\\ u_1 & u_2 & u_3\\ v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \end{vmatrix}.$$ (computational form) :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
2.4 The Cross Product
Magnitude (Area)
$$||\mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v}||=||\mathbf{u}||\,||\mathbf{v}||\sin\theta,$$ the **area of parallelogram** with sides $\mathbf{u},\mathbf{v}$. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
2.4 The Cross Product
Special Properties
If $\mathbf{u}\times\mathbf{v}=0$, then $\mathbf{u},\mathbf{v}$ are **parallel** (zero area). Triple scalar product: $$\mathbf{u}\cdot(\mathbf{v}\times\mathbf{w})$$ gives **volume** of parallelepiped. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
