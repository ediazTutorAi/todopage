MATH 2326 · Dr. Esteban Diaz
Definition
Limit of a Function of Two Variables
Let $f$ be a function of two variables, x and y. The limit of $f$ as $(x,y)$ approaches $(a,b)$ is L, written: $$\lim_{{(x,y) \to (a,b)}} f(x,y) = L$$ if for every $\epsilon > 0$ there exists a $\delta > 0$ such that for all points $(x,y)$ in a $\delta$ disk around $(a,b)$, except possible for $(a,b)$ itself, the value of $f(x,y)$ is no more than $\epsilon$ away from L. Using symbols, we write the following: For any $\epsilon > 0$, there exists a $\delta > 0$ such that: $$|f(x,y)-L|<\epsilon$$ whenever $0<\sqrt{(x-a)^2+(y-b)^2}<\delta$.
