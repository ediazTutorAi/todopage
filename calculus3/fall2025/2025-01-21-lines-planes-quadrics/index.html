Theorem 2.11
Parametric and Symmetric Equations of a Line
A line L parallel to vector $\mathbf{v}=\langle a,b,c\rangle$ and passing through the point $P_0(x_0,y_0,z_0)$ can be described by the following parametric equations: $$ x=x_0+at\,y=y_0+bt\,z=z_0+ct $$ If the constants $a,b,c$ are all nonzero, then the line can also be described by the following symmetric equations: $$ \frac{x-x_0}{a}=\frac{y-y_0}{b}=\frac{z-z_0}{c} $$ Observation: The parametric equations of a line are not unique. For example, if we choose a different point on the line, we will get different parametric equations. Similarly, if we choose a different direction vector, we will get different parametric equations.

Parametric and Symmetric Equations of a Line
Let $P_1(x_1,y_1,z_1)$ and $P_2(x_2,y_2,z_2)$ be two points in $\mathbb{R}^3$. The line segment between $P_1$ and $P_2$ is given by the following parametric equations: $$x=x_1+(x_2-x_1)t\ y=y_1+(y_2-y_1)t\ z=z_1+(z_2-z_1)t$$ where $0 \leq t \leq 1$.

Definition
Cylinder, rulings, and trace
A set of lines parallel to a given lines passing through a given curve is called a cylinder. The parallel lines are called rulings.

The trace of a surface are the the cross-sections created when the surface intersects a plane parallel to one of the coordinate planes. The traces are curves.
Definition
Quadric Surfaces
A quadric surface is a surface that can be described by a second-degree equation in three variables. The general form of a quadric surface is given by: $$Ax^2+By^2+Cz^2+Dxy+Exz+Fyz+Gx+Hy+Jz+K=0$$ Show the pictures from the book.
