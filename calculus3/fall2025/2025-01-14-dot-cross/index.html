Dot and Cross Products

MATH 2326 · Dr. Esteban Diaz
2.3 Vectors Page 1
Dot Product (Algebraic Definition)
For $\vec{u}=\langle u_1,u_2,u_3\rangle$ and $\vec{v}=\langle v_1,v_2,v_3\rangle$, $$\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v}=u_1v_1+u_2v_2+u_3v_3.$$
2.3 Vectors Page 2
Dot Product (Geometric Definition)
$$\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v}=||\vec{u}||\,||\vec{v}||\cos\theta,$$ where $\theta$ is the angle between $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$.
2.3 Vectors Page 3
Orthogonality
$\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ are orthogonal iff $$\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v}=0.$$
2.3 Vectors Page 4
Projection
The projection of $\vec{u}$ onto $\vec{v}$ is $$\text{proj}_{\vec{v}}\vec{u}=\frac{\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v}}{||\vec{v}||^2}\vec{v}.$$
2.4 Vectors Page 1
Cross Product (Definition)
$\vec{u}\times\vec{v}$ is a vector orthogonal to both $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$, with direction given by the right-hand rule.
2.4 Vectors Page 2
Cross Product (Magnitude)
$$||\vec{u}\times\vec{v}||=||\vec{u}||\,||\vec{v}||\sin\theta,$$ equal to the area of the parallelogram spanned by $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$.
2.4 Vectors Page 3
Determinant Formula
$$\vec{u}\times\vec{v}= \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ u_1 & u_2 & u_3 \\ v_1 & v_2 & v_3 \end{vmatrix}.$$
2.4 Vectors Page 4
Parallel Vectors
$\vec{u}\times\vec{v}=\vec{0}$ iff $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ are parallel.
2.4 Vectors Page 5
Area Applications
Area of a parallelogram: $$A=||\vec{u}\times\vec{v}||.$$ Area of a triangle: $$A=\tfrac12||\vec{u}\times\vec{v}||.$$
