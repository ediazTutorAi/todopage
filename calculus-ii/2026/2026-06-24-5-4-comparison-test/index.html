5.4 Comparison Test

Objectives
0. The p-series

1. Use the comparison test to test a series for convergence
2. Use limit comparison test to test a series for convergence

p-series
The series of the form \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{n^p}\) converges when \(p>1\). Otherwise diverges.

Proof: By the Integral Test.
Comparison Test
i. Suppose there exists an integer N such that \(0\leq a_n\leq b_n\) for all \(n\geq N\). If \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n\) converges, then \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n\) converges.

ii. Suppose there exists an integer N such that \(a_n\geq b_n \geq 0\) for all \(n\geq N\). If \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} b_n\) diverges, then \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n\) diverges.

Proof: Use the Bounded Monotone Convergence Theorem
Example Comparison Test
Suppose that \(\sum a_n\) and \(\sum b_n\) are series with positive terms for all n, and the series terms satisfies the next conditions. What can you say about the convergence and divergence of the series? \[a_n\leq\frac{1}{n^{3/2}}\leq b_n\]
Example Integral Test
Use the Integral Test to find the convergence or divergence of this series \[\sum_{n=2}^{\infty}ne^{-n}\]
Example Comparison Test
Analize the series \(\displaystyle\sum_{k=2}^{\infty} \frac{\sqrt{lnk}}{k}\) for convergence and divergence.
Example Integral Test
Use the integral test to analize the series \(\displaystyle\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\frac{k}{e^k}\)
