5.5 Alternating Series

Objectives
i. Use the alternating series test to test an alternating series for convergence

ii. Estimate the sum of an alternating series

ii. Explain the meaning of absolute convergence and conditional convergence
Alternating Series
Any series whose terms alternate between positive and negative values is called an alternating series. An alternating series can be written in the form \[\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(-1)^{n+1}b_n=b_1-b_2+b_3-b_4+\cdots\]or \[\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(-1)^nb_n=-b_1+b_2-b_3+\cdots\]where \(b_n>0\) for all positive integers n.
Alternating Series Test
An alternating series converges if

\(0 < b_{n+1}\leq b_n\) and

ii. \(\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty}b_n=0\)

b) \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(-1)^{n+1}\frac{n}{n+1}\)
